Booking visit

First trimester — full medical history and tailored care plan.

Booking visit

First trimester
  • Blood group and antibody screen
  • Full blood count
  • Urine test
  • Infections in pregnancy — syphilis, HIV, Hepatitis B, Hepatitis C, Rubella
  • Iron levels
  • Early diabetes testing
  • Thyroid function
  • CMV and varicella antibodies
  • Dating ultrasound
  • Cervical screening
  • Genetic screening
  • Fetal DNA (for anti-D planning)

Antenatal visits

Raise your birth, labour, and postnatal preferences at every visit.

20–22 week visit

Second trimester
  • Morphology ultrasound — a detailed check of your baby's development, position, the placenta and cervix
  • Whooping cough vaccine (20–32 weeks)
  • Influenza vaccine (offered any time during pregnancy)

28–30 week visit

Third trimester
  • Repeat blood counts
  • Blood group and antibodies
  • Syphilis serology
  • Oral glucose tolerance test (diabetes check)
  • Whooping cough vaccine (20–32 weeks)
  • RSV vaccine (28–32 weeks)
  • Influenza vaccine (any time)
  • STI screening — chlamydia, gonorrhoea
  • Iron stores
  • Growth ultrasound (may be offered 28–32 weeks)

32–34 week visit

Third trimester
  • Labour signs and preparing for birth
  • Preparing for baby
  • Sleep quality
  • Mental health
  • Social and emotional wellbeing
  • Antenatal expressing
  • Breastfeeding and alternatives

36 week visit

Third trimester
  • Repeat blood counts
  • Group B streptococcus swab
  • Growth ultrasound (usually 32–36 weeks, based on risk factors)
  • Iron stores

40 week visit

Third trimester
  • Induction of labour options if pregnancy continues past your due date
  • Checking your baby's wellbeing

Labour and birth

Think about who you want with you and what you'd like discussed before labour begins.

  • Monitoring your baby's heartbeat
  • Internal examinations
  • Antibiotics if needed
  • Assistance with the birth if required
  • Pain relief options

Postnatal care

After the birth.

Immediately after birth

Minutes to hours
  • Skin-to-skin contact
  • Time alone with your baby
  • How you plan to feed your baby
  • Newborn vaccinations — Hepatitis B and Vitamin K
  • Having your partner or support person with you

First days after birth

  • Visiting arrangements for your partner and family
  • Going home planning
  • Mental health and emotional wellbeing care plan

Going home

First weeks to months
  • Managing at home and who is available to support you
  • Feeding support
  • Ongoing mental health care
  • Follow-up appointments for you and your baby
